The video below presents a graphic illustration of the collaborative filtering algorithm used by TwinkedIn. The gray nodes represent all Twitter identities retrieved by searching for the user's LinkedIn contacts by name on Twitter. The problem is that each search returns more than one result (approximately 15 on average). The basic assumption is then that nodes are more likely to correspond to the correct identities in the user's LinkedIn network if they are connected to one another through 'following' or 'follower' relationships. The collaborative algorithm uses these relationships to cluster connected nodes and to 'pull' them out of the pool of all possible nodes. This process disambiguates them from the unconnected nodes that remain in the middle and that are less likely to correspond to the identities looked for.

TwinkedIn is the result of Dominique Van de Sompel's project at the Insight Data Science Fellows program.
